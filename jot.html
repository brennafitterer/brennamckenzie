Jot

A digital organization platform. No more are the days of switching between multiple platforms to reference your digital assets, now you can collaborate, share and edit your notes, images, and links all in one space.

Project overview

Problem

Solution

A website designed for the user derived from data.

My Role

Research, information architecture, testing, branding, and design.

Tools

Illustrator, Sketch, Usability Hub, InVision

Testing

Once the design was in place, additional testing was required to ensure the design elements that were applied to the wireframes continued to resonate with users. To do this I conducted several preference tests to guide the direction of the design.


Tested the placement of the add button within the dashboard to understand which placement was most intuitive for users when adding a new piece of content to the dashboard.



Tested the home page design to understand if users preferred scrolling below the fold to access details about the product or if they preferred using top navigating to access that same information.

Features & functions

I used the data gathered from user research and user testing to establish the final features and functions for Jot’s dashboard. The layout was strategic for easy adoption as it aligns with the structure of various email platforms.

Conclusion

Jot solves a real problem for people who are looking to save, share and write digital content. With the cloud storage and organization industry growing, I believe Jot provides a real differentiator by creating a collaborative space for users with an easy to adopt design. By creating a product that's design aligns with various email platforms it makes for easy adoption for new users creating a good user experience.

In considering future development of the site, I would like to further address some of the needs requested by users to incorporate informational videos, more robust collaboration opportunities (real time) and exporting functionality.

I learned a lot while developing this project. The main take away though was that data is key to the success of designing for the users and not to your assumptions. I encountered several instances where I assumed users would prefer one design and through testing, they choose the other. This project just reinforced that testing throughout the design process is how you will be able to create the most successful website and product design.
