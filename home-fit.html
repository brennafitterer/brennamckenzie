Home Fit

Are you tired of paying for a gym membership? Do you want to be able to work out at your own convenience? Home Fit can help you build the gym you need to reach your fitness goals. Whether you need simple tools to enhance your body weight workout or a fitness center, we can help you transform your home into your ideal fitness space.

Project overview

Problem

People are looking for a variety of ways to workout and taking the workout to the home is on the rise. There are not a lot of options out there for purchasing equipment for the home, let alone responsive sites that allow you to easily search, compare, and purchase new or used equipment for all types of workouts.

Solution

A responsive website that allows users to easily search, compare, and purchase new or pre-owned workout equipment for their home ranging from bodyweight equipment to strength and conditioning.

My Role

Conceptualize, research, information architecture, testing, branding, and design.

Tools

Figma, Sketch, Usability Hub, InVision

Assumptions

People want the option to work out from home and need equipment to support their workouts


People want to be able to purchase new or pre-owned workout equipment from one place


People want to be able to compare different equipment prior to purchasing

Competitor analysis

Data-driven design starts with knowing your space. I researched five competitors to better understand my competition and establish my differentiators.

Dick’s Sporting Goods, has a very reputable brand making it easy for users to visit their site. Given they sell more than just workout equipment they have a robust navigation system, making it hard to find the section you are looking for. Once in the section, you have to perform multiple clicks prior to viewing products and their filtration system is not as robust.

Fitness Giant has good UX/UI elements such as the way they categorize the products, search features, bread crumbs and the use of images explaining how to use the products. They could improve by simplifying the navigation, adding in filters, simplifying the UI and reducing clicks.

Life Fitness has a very clean and elevated UI in comparison to other sites. They have a simple navigation, ability to compare products and both home and gym equipment. They could improve the user experience through quick preview options and by simplifying the navigation further.

NordicTrack has a very clean and beautiful UI, but the UX is not as user-friendly. You have to make numerous clicks to get to the product and then the product page requires quite a bit of scrolling to obtain information about the product which is not easily scalable in one area.

Colorado home fitness provides good information on the benefits of home fitness and a good UX for displaying their products. They don’t allow you to easily buy all products online, hard to find main navigation and design feels cluttered.

Key findings

  1. My target audience does indeed workout and avidly. They are also potentially interested in buying workout equipment as the majority do workout at home or would be interested in it.
  2. 89% of users are interested in purchasing both new and pre-owned workout equipment, which verifies my assumption and brings to light one of our core differentiators.
  3. 96% of users want to be able to compare products prior to purchasing which validates my original assumption.
  4. Users are interested in purchasing a variety of types of equipment, so specializing in just conditioning equipment is not the route to go.
  5. Gathered key data to help structure my personas, structure of the site and features and functions.

User flow

I developed the user flow to understand how users will engage with the site, establish the core actions we want users to take on the site, and outline each of those flows. Based on the data gathered during user research, there are at least five key user flows that we would want our users to engage with, but there is one main that I focused on which is the purchase process.

View user flow

User journey

Search

  • Filter by workout level or type of equipment
  • Filter by muscle group, new or pre-owned, product category or price
  • Search for equipment
  • Equipment recommendations
  • Purchasing equipment best practices

Shopping

  • Compare products
  • Filter by muscle group, new or pre-owned, product category or price
  • Login
  • Favorite equipment
  • View ratings
  • View reviews
  • Quick preview

Purchase

  • Purchase equipment
  • Pay with PayPal, Sign in/Sign up or Pay as a guest

Wireframe

I then used the wireframes to create a prototype and conduct user testing to improve the flow, features, and functionality of the site to ensure it aligned with the target audience prior to moving into design.

Testing

Once the design was in place, additional testing was required to ensure the design elements that were applied to the wireframes continued to resonate with users. To do this I conducted several preference tests to guide the direction of the design.


My goal for the mobile view was to be able to eliminate the need for a navigation menu. I conducted user testing to ensure that including the navigation capabilities within the body of the page and pulling the search functionality out of a hamburger menu was still intuitive for users to be able to navigate and search for items within the site. The results showed that users used the navigation within the body of the site and gravitated toward the search icon over the hamburger for searching.




Tested the call-to-actions on the home page to understand how users wanted to get started searching by workout level.

Features & functions

I used the data gathered from user research and user testing to establish the final features and functions for Home Fit. The layout and capabilities were strategic for easy adoption as it is simple and straightforward. I wanted to ensure with the design that users had a great experience so they would align with our desired user flow.

Main pages
