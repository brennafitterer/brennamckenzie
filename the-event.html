The Event

People love to be entertained. No matter what your pleasure, it is always nice to have a night out on the town. With one central platform to locate the event of the night, you will come to rely on The Event for all your entertainment needs from sports, to theater, to music and more, The Event is where you find the best tickets and prices in town.

View prototype

Project overview

Problem

Invent, design, and brand a mobile application.

Solution

A mobile application designed for the user derived from data.

My Role

Conceptualize, research, information architecture, testing, branding, and design.

Tools

Illustrator, Sketch, Usability Hub, InVision

Assumptions

Users want to be able to easily find tickets for a variety of different genres of events all in one app


Users want to be able to find different types of events (mainstream, underground, up-and-coming) all in one app


Users want to be able to price compare tickets for events in one app

Competitor analysis

Data-driven design starts with knowing your space. I researched five competitors to better understand my competition and establish my differentiators.

Tickets to music, festivals, and sports events. They have a very clean UI, but it is hard to find their filter capabilities and they only provide tickets to mainstream events.

Search engine that allows you to find cheap flights. Nice clean UI for price comparing tickets across multiple platforms.

Sitemap

Developed the structure of the app to include all features and functions that were identified in the discovery and strategy phase. Mapping out the structure of the app really helps to visualize all the pages that are required for the app, how features could be structured and the amount of work it would take to launch the app for both designers and stakeholders.

User flow

I developed the user flow to understand how users will engage with the app. Based on the data gathered during user research, it is a continuous flow that users will be in as we want them to continue to come back and search for events, purchase tickets, and access purchased tickets.

Search

  • Filter by genre category
  • Filter by date, price, location
  • Search for events
  • View events on a calendar
  • View events on a map
  • Event recommendations

Shopping

  • Price compare tickets
  • Filter by date, price, location
  • Login
  • Favorite events
  • Share events

Purchase

  • Purchase tickets
  • Notifications
  • Add to calendar

Tickets

  • Sell tickets
  • Trade tickets
  • Status of selling & transferring tickets
  • Purchased tickets
  • Past tickets

Wireframe

I translated my data, sitemap, user flow, and sketches into hi-fidelity wireframes to better understand the structure and content that should be incorporated on each page. I then used the wireframes to create a prototype and conduct user testing to improve the flow, features, and functionality of the app to ensure it aligned with the target audience prior to moving into design.

Brand

The brand is intended to have a fun, sophisticated, and innovative feel so it appeals to the target audience of those who frequent events, to those who go sparingly, and all those in between. The logo, font type, and colors were chosen to align with that esthetic so The Event feels professional, fun, and easy to use.

Conclusion

When creating The Event I set out to develop a mobile application where the functionalities are based on the validation of assumptions from user research. Once I was able to validate those assumptions, I designed an interface to align with the needs of our users based on user testing.

If this were a real mobile application, I would continue to evolve the features and functions based on the user data I collected throughout the design process and once the application was live. Those additions would include:

  • Making events recommendations more prevalent within the app by including them at the bottom of event pages and at the top of the popular section
  • Add in the capability for users to add events to the app
  • Add a social component to the app where you can follow other users, share pictures, etc.
  • Add in location and event ratings based on past events users have attended

I learned a lot while developing this app. Making sure to adhere to mobile guidelines, not settling for your first iteration of design and making sure your brand assets align with your target audience is key to having a successful app.
